We all know that the population in some countries is much larger than others. But just how large are they compared to others?
I wanted to find out so I gathered all the countries population on a spreadsheat and used a data map to find out what the difference really looks like. We can see that the top five largest countries are: China, India, USA, Indonesia, and Brazil.

But, what are the smallest five countries? Starting from the smallest and moving up they are: Holy See(799), Tokelau(1,340), Niue(1,628), Falkland Islands(2,921), and Saint Helena(4,096).



After finding out all the population I got curious on about if the median age was higher in the countries with a higher population or if it isn't true.
It turns out the population number is not in a correlation to the median age. China and the USA have lower median age than countriess who have a lower population.
